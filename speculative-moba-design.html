Speculative MOBA Design
HCI Case STudy
01 —
background

This project was a group assignment for IAT 201 (Human-Computer Interaction and Cognition) during Spring 2022, particularly in relation to projecting, smart-seeing, and close-coupling in MOBA video games.

Contributions: League of Legends and Wild Rift research, gameplay design, and interface design

The team included other SIAT students⁠— Alexandra Ileto, Jalene Pang, and Sapol Pittayakornpisuth.

What is projecting, smart-seeing, and close-coupling?

02 —
approach
Design Objective

How can we use smart-seeing, projecting, and close-coupling to design a MOBA interface for mobile with a simple learning curve while maintaining a complex and rewarding gameplay?

Research Questions
  • Since MOBAs are character-dependent, is skill in piloting characters more important than overall game strategy? (micro vs macro)
  • What current aspects of MOBAs keep players engaged through the use of close-coupling?
  • What kinds of features help to maintain player attention and engagement in MOBA interfaces?
Research Analysis

After playing a variety of MOBA games such as League of Legends (+ Wild Rift), Brawl Stars, and Clash Royale, our team determined that common factors within MOBAs is that it requires many repetitive tasks that can make the gameplay dull over time, contains a lack of varied interaction due to the lack of close-coupling employed by players, and that initial games may feel slow to uninitiated players.

Hypothesis

If more interactive/dynamic external representations are present in the video game, then the player will be more engaged in the gameplay because close-coupling, smart-seeing, and projecting skills would be required.

03 —
PROPOSAL

  • A simple and comprehensible UI layout and controls
  • Addition of PvE elements that are accessible to all the players, allowing them to deviate from the repetitive battle set up
  • Provide basic tactical advice to motivate players with a sense of learning
  • High incentives for high-risk objectives to increase engagement from both winning or losing states (like the golden snitch in Harry Potter)

04 —
FINDINGS
Upsides

Players would be encouraged to progress as the games remain competitive between teams, allowing players to employ close-coupling in addition to the enhancement of individual skill expression and theorycrafting.

Downsides

Because of added PvE interactive elements and provided tactical advice, gameplay would initially lack competitiveness and development of the players' own strategies may be hindered.

05 —
Reflections

The results of our research illustrated the difficulty of creating a MOBA game for specifically casual players. Though it is important to employ cognitive practices, differences in skill gap will always be present in MOBA games, and what is most important is to ensure fair play by matching players with the same skills.

Our team collaborated on this case study from varying gaming backgrounds. After many discussions with other group members, I learned the importance of employing distributed cognition practices to create accessible designs for games and otherwise.

In this century, a majority of people in online spaces already know what they are looking for and how to find it! Having consistencies within various design systems can facilitate a straightforward user experience.
