Exit Project
BOOOOOOOM (Speculative Design)
MICROSITE DESIGN
01 —
background

This was the final project for IAT 235 (Information Design) during Summer 2022. The task was to create a speculative microsite for an already-existing website, using design practices learned throughout the semester.

Contributions: UX/UI research and design, prototyping, microsite building
Tools: Figma, Webflow

The team included other SIAT students⁠— YuFeng Diao, Baolati Pazeli, Yujia Wan, and Zak Zastera.

The team's chosen website was www.booooooom.com— "an authoritative voice in the new contemporary arts scene, highlighting emerging talents, and launching the careers of many young artists internationally".

02 —
PROPOSAL

We wanted to focus on up-and-coming photographers as our user group:
new photographers who are seeking exposure and/or prompts for inspiration.

Our booooooom microsite would hold online contests where this specific user group can submit photographs to get the chance to win and be highlighted on a winner profile page. Contest would have a combination of specific rules to keep it fair across all levels of photography skill.

03 —
DESIGN DECISIONS
  • Incorporate black and white, minimalism, and swiss design for a clean and readable interface which allows photographs to be always highlighted.
  • A landing page gallery that displays multiple rotating images to allow users to glance through many photographs simultaneously, with the ability to click on any that may catch their eye.
  • Use grids for the photographs to show a spaced-out yet united visual.
04 —
REFLECTIONS

It was exciting to create a microsite albeit speculatively because I was able to operate under an iterative design process with my team and learn how to collectively navigate through issues to create an accessible design.

I learned the necessity of empathizing with users to understand their needs in favour of knowing what the user experience should be.
