Key to Your Heart
SHORT FILM
01 —
background

"Key to Your Heart" is the product of a collaborative effort between four students as the final project for IAT 202 (New Media Images) during Spring 2022.

Contributions: Director, Writer, Director of Photography, and Soundtrack
Software Used: Adobe Audition, Final Cut Pro, Logic Pro
Equipment: Canon EOS Rebel T7i, Zoom H4n

The team included other SIAT students⁠— Junniel Abeto, Calista Alex, and Stella Faustin.

Screening Awards: Best Actor, Best Drama, Best Comedy, Best Editing, and Audience Choice

02 —
PRE-PRODUCTION

Our team unanimously agreed to create a romantic comedy as it was a genre we all enjoyed to watch. I volunteered to draft the script over reading break after spending some time together with the team on Discord to brainstorm and watch other films for inspiration.

After the script was approved by the teaching staff, coco panda productions was born and we began to collectively draft a shot list, storyboard, mood board, and production schedule.

03 —
PRODUCTION

Our initial plan was to film outdoors, but we had to transition our set to indoors to accommodate the weather for our available shooting dates.

The first day of filming began with a table read and we proceeded to shoot the entire film as a rehearsal to hash out any errors and to have a rough cut ready to receive constructive feedback across all aspects of production.

04 —
RE-SHOOTS

After a week of team discussions and revisions revolved around feedback and critique we received that week, our team was able to re-shoot the entire film!

Editing was finalized early due to the feedback we received, and I spent the remaining few days before the deadline to finalize the soundtrack, which includes an original instrumental from my personal music project.

PRODUCTION STILLS
06 —
REFLECTIONS

Through the creation of this film, I learned the importance of constantly creating different, adaptable iterations to find the best solution, and, as the director, how to coalesce the team to work together in harmony.

Despite the many challenges our team encountered using camera and especially sound equipment while creating our first short film, it has guided us to remember the critical aspects of how to create quality films, leaving us prepared for the next one.
